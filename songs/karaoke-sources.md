## üì• Downloading & Preparing Tracks

### Step 1: Download

**From YouTube:**
- Use: youtube-dl, 4K Video Downloader, or online converters
- Format: MP3 or WAV
- Quality: Highest available

**From paid services:**
- Direct download from website
- Usually MP3 or WAV format

### Step 2: Trim to 20-30 Seconds

**Tools:**
- **Online:** mp3cut.net, audiotrimmer.com (free, easy)
- **Software:** Audacity (free, more control)
- **Mobile:** Audio Editor apps

**How to choose section:**
1. Find the most recognizable part (usually chorus)
2. Ensure it includes key lyrics that match character
3. Start and end at natural musical phrases
4. Aim for 20-30 seconds total

**Example - "Creep" by Radiohead:**
- Best section: 0:58-1:18 (20 seconds)
- Includes: "I'm a creep, I'm a weirdo..."
- Most iconic part of song

### Step 3: Organize Files

**Naming convention:**
```
[song-title]_[artist]_karaoke.mp3
```

**Examples:**
- `creep_radiohead_karaoke.mp3`
- `youre-so-vain_carly-simon_karaoke.mp3`

**Save to:** `assets/audio/karaoke/`

## üö® Legal Considerations

### **Using Karaoke Tracks:**

**Generally OK:**
- Using instrumental tracks (no original vocals)
- Adding AI-generated vocals
- Short clips (20-30 seconds)
- Transformative/parody use
- Proper attribution

**Check:**
- Platform terms of service (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram)
- Copyright claims on specific songs
- Commercial vs. personal use

**Safest approach:**
- Use licensed karaoke tracks (Karaoke Version)
- Keep clips short (under 30 seconds)
- Transformative content (comedy/parody)
- Monitor for copyright claims

**Not legal advice** - consult lawyer if concerned
